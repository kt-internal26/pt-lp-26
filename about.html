Our Story

How It All Began

In 2008, a small group of passionate volunteers gathered in a modest community hall with a single purpose: to respond to the suffering they saw around them. Families struggling without food, children unable to afford school, villagers walking miles for basic medical care — the needs were immense, and the response was simple: we must help.

"We didn't have much when we started. But we had belief — belief that ordinary people, working together, could do extraordinary things."
— Founder, Pratap Chandra Das Memorial Trust

Starting with a single literacy program serving 40 children, the Foundation grew steadily — one village at a time, one family at a time. Donors near and far contributed their resources. Local volunteers gave their time. And communities, once forgotten, began to see hope on the horizon.

Today, Pratap Chandra Das Memorial Trust runs more than 12 active programs across 32 villages, touching over 12,000 lives annually. Our work spans free education support, mobile healthcare camps, vocational training for women, and emergency relief during natural disasters.

But the numbers, as meaningful as they are, tell only part of the story. The real story is in the child who now attends college on a scholarship we provided. In the mother who started her own small business with our micro-finance support. In the farmer who has clean water because we helped build a community well. These are the stories that fuel us, day after day.
